Meet Our Founder

"Resilience and self-confidence saved my life."

Celestina’s story is inseparable from the organisation she built. After surviving severe childhood trauma and a spinal injury at 16 that left her temporarily paralysed, she taught herself to walk again and developed a profound sense of purpose around healing, family and human dignity.

Today she is a wife, mother and fully qualified integrative behaviour psychotherapist with 15 years in practice. Her mission is to help parents heal their own wounds so they do not unknowingly pass them on to their children.

Our Journey

From personal conviction to community sanctuary

What began as a deep commitment to healing has grown into a multidisciplinary organisation that bridges therapy, social support and community action.

7+years of dedicated service as FCC, built on earlier decades of work
500+lives re-anchored through trauma-informed support and mentoring
24/7global access through diaspora-facing support and international outreach
2001-2005

Early inspiration

Celestina’s journey starts in early years education, inspired by her father’s nursery and a growing passion for children and young people.

2006-2012

Therapy and youth work

She qualifies as a psychotherapist and supports young people and elderly communities through organisations including Off the Record Croydon, Place2Be and MIND Leyton.

2013-2014

The vision takes shape

Homelessness work in Redbridge informs the first food-bank and wraparound support model, leading to the founding of Floating Counselling.

2016-2018

Community wellbeing expands

Weekly food support, retreats, mentoring and intergenerational programmes begin to connect therapy with broader stability and empowerment.

2019-Present

A global sanctuary

FCC grows into a community-facing organisation with anti-knife crime work, Homeless Necessity outreach, NHS partnerships, holiday school programmes and international support pathways.
